Free bus pass (Diamond Travelcard)

You’re entitled to a free bus pass if you’re a BANES resident and have reached the qualifying age or have certain disabilities. The bus pass is called a Diamond Travelcard.

How to use your bus pass

You must show your pass to the driver every time you get on a bus. Some bus companies will issue you with a ticket. If so, please keep the ticket until you get off the bus as you may need to show it to an inspector. If you forget to take your pass with you, you must pay the normal fare.

Bath tour bus services

You can use your bus pass to obtain a free Shuttle ticket to make local journeys between stops along the route. The Shuttle ticket is only valid for seats on the lower deck of the bus. Headphones for the tour commentary will not be provided. The bus pass is not valid for the purchase of sightseeing tickets.

Dial-a-Ride services

If you are registered with a local Dial-a-Ride group, your bus pass entitles you to discounted fares. For full details, please contact the group.

Airport Flyer services

You can use your bus pass for free travel on the A4 route between Bath and the Airport Tavern (except between 4am and 9am on Monday to Friday). However, you’ll be charged a child fare to travel from the Airport Tavern to Bristol Airport.

Bus passes are not accepted for free off-peak travel on bus service A1 (Bristol – Bristol Airport).

Exclusions

Your bus pass is not valid on:

  • Bath City Tour or Skyline Tour (except short local journeys)
  • Bristol Open-Top Tour
  • Long-distance coach services
  • Rail and ferry services
  • Tram, Underground and Metro services
  • Local bus services in Scotland, Northern Ireland, Isle of Man, Channel Islands and Wales (except for the direct bus service between Bristol or South Gloucestershire and Chepstow, Newport or places in between)
  • Taxis
If you lose or damage your pass

If you’ve lost your bus pass, it’s a good idea to check with bus operators’ lost property offices if you cannot find it anywhere else. If it is lost, or you have damaged your pass, go to a council One Stop Shop and we’ll cancel it and issue you with a replacement while you wait. There is a non-refundable charge for replacing a lost or damaged pass. If you later find your lost pass, hand it in to a council One Stop Shop.

If your pass is stolen

If your pass has been stolen, the theft must be reported to the police. They will give you a Crime Reference Number which we will need in order to issue a replacement. Stolen passes are cancelled and cannot be used for travel. If your stolen pass is subsequently returned to you, please hand it in at a council One Stop Shop.

Changing the photograph on your bus pass

If you need to change the photograph on your bus pass, you can visit a council One Stop Shop to have a new photograph taken. A new pass will be produced with the updated photograph on it. If the reason for the change is because your appearance has changed significantly, then this service is free of charge. Otherwise there will be a charge.

How to re-apply for an older person’s bus pass when it expires

Your bus pass is valid for 3 years. If you still live in the Bath and North East Somerset area, we’ll renew your bus pass automatically. If you’ve changed your address in the last 3 years, please ensure that your address held on our Council Tax records is up-to-date. We’ll send your new pass a few weeks before your old pass expires. Please destroy your old pass or return it to a council One Stop Shop.

If you haven’t received your new pass 7 days before the expiry of your old one, please visit a council One Stop Shop. We’ll check your details and issue you with a new pass while you wait.

How to re-apply for a disabled person’s bus pass when it expires

Your bus pass is valid for 3 years. If you still live in the Bath and North East Somerset area, we’ll need to confirm that you’re still eligible. You’ll receive a letter from us before your bus pass expires. You’ll be asked to visit your nearest council One Stop Shop with evidence that you still have a qualifying disability. We’ll then issue your new pass and you can start using it immediately. Please destroy your old pass or return it to a council One Stop Shop.

What you need to know

  • If you allow someone else to use your bus pass, it could be withdrawn
  • Your bus pass gives you no more rights than any other passenger
  • If you move within the council area, tell us your new address as soon as possible
  • If you move out of the council area, you’ll need to apply to your new council for a replacement pass
  • If you no longer require a pass, please return it to a council One Stop Shop
  • If you are applying for several passes on behalf of a group or organisation, you’ll need to make an appointment in advance by emailing triage@bathnes.gov.uk
