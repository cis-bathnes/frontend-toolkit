You may be eligible for a bus pass.

How to get your bus pass

To get your bus pass, you must bring proof of your eligibility to the One Stop Shop in Bath, Keynsham or Midsomer Norton. We’ll check your details, take your photo free of charge and then print your card while you wait.

What you must bring:

Proof of eligibility

You will need to meet the definition of learning disability used by the Department for Transport (DfT):

"A person with a learning disability has a reduced ability to understand new or complex information, a difficulty in learning new skills, and may be unable to cope independently. These disabilities must have started before adulthood and have a lasting effect on development. The person should be able to qualify for specialist services and he or she may have had special educational provision."

You will need to provide:

  1. Confirmation from a medical specialist that you have a learning disability
  2. A description of the medical condition or learning disability including any medical terms
  3. Confirmation that the disabilities started before adulthood and have a lasting effect on development
! Warning Conditions such as depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, dyslexia, and ADHD are not eligible for a disabled person’s bus pass unless they meet one of the other criteria.

Proof of residence in Bath and North East Somerset

Please bring one of these (no more than 6 months old):

  • a council tax bill
  • a utility bill
  • a copy of your tenancy agreement
  • a bank statement
  • evidence of your entitlement to vote in Bath and North East Somerset
For boat dwellers only

Please bring one of these:

  • a mooring licence for a site in Bath and North East Somerset
  • a utility bill
  • evidence of your entitlement to vote in Bath and North East Somerset
  • a letter from a government department or public body addressed to you at a PO Box or Poste Restante in Bath and North East Somerset

Proof of identity

Please bring one of these:

  • birth certificate or adoption certificate
  • marriage or divorce certificate
  • passport
  • civil partnership or dissolution certificate
  • valid photo driving licence (both parts)

For those applying on behalf of a person under 16

Please bring one of these:

  • NHS medical card/letter
  • The child’s bank or building society statement
  • a DWP or HMRC letter detailing the child’s name and address
!WarningWe do not issue bus passes for children under 5 as they do not pay on buses.
