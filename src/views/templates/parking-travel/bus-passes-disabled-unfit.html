You may be eligible for a bus pass if you would be refused a driving licence on medical grounds. Reasons for this might include

  • Epilepsy
  • Severe mental disorder
  • Liability to sudden attacks of dizziness or fainting
  • Unable to read a number plate in good light at 20.5 metres, with lenses if worn
  • You have another disability or condition that would make driving a vehicle a danger to the public

You will need to provide evidence from a medical specialist that confirm your condition. You may be charged for obtaining this.

If the DVLA has revoked your licence on medical grounds you’ll need to provide a copy of the letter they sent you.

You will need to take your documents into one of our One Stop Shops and we will process your application whilst you wait.

! Warning People who misuse drugs and alcohol are not eligible for a disabled person’s bus pass
