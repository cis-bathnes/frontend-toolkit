Apply for a disabled person’s bus pass

To get a disabled person’s bus pass (Diamond Travelcard), you must be a Bath and North East Somerset resident and have a qualifying disability.

Check your eligibility

You may qualify if you:

  • are blind or partially sighted
  • are deaf
  • are without speech
  • are unable to walk
  • have no arms or lack of use of arms
  • have a learning disability
  • are unfit to drive (but not because of alcohol or drugs)
  • are a Blue Badge holder
  • receive the War Pensioner’s Mobility Supplement
  • receive the Higher Rate Mobility Component of Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • receive Personal Independence Payment (PIP), where you have been awarded at least eight points against either the “Moving around” and/or “Communicating verbally” activities

Check Proof of qualifying disability for more information.

How to get your bus pass

To get your bus pass, you must bring proof of your eligibility to the One Stop Shop in Bath, Keynsham or Midsomer Norton. We’ll check your details, take your photo free of charge and then print your card while you wait.

What you must bring

Proof of residence in Bath and North East Somerset

Please bring one of these (no more than 6 months old):

  • a council tax bill
  • a utility bill
  • a copy of your tenancy agreement
  • a bank statement
  • evidence of your entitlement to vote in Bath and North East Somerse
For boat dwellers only

Please bring one of these:

  • a mooring licence for a site in Bath and North East Somerset
  • a utility bill
  • evidence of your entitlement to vote in Bath and North East Somerset
  • a letter from a government department or public body addressed to you at a PO Box or Poste Restante in Bath and North East Somerset

Proof of identity

Please bring one of these:

  • birth certificate or adoption certificate
  • marriage or divorce certificate
  • passport
  • civil partnership or dissolution certificate
  • valid photo driving licence (both parts)

For those applying on behalf of a person under 16

Please bring one of these:

  • NHS medical card/letter
  • The child’s bank or building society statement
  • a DWP or HMRC letter detailing the child’s name and address
