{{Title}}

If your business premises is in a residents parking zone, you may be entitled to up to 2 parking permits for your customers to use when visiting.

Check eligibility and apply

Please note that you cannot apply for a business parking permit if

  • your premesis has any off-street parking
  • you are located in the Bath central zone
  • your building was constructed or converted after 2006

Mixing with other types of parking permit

These permits, together with essential business vehicle permits and any residents parking permits count towards your total maximum of 2 permits for your premesis. So for example you could apply for 1 essential vehicle and 1 customer permit, but not 2 of each.

How to use

Give the paper permit to your customer when they arrive and ask them to display it clearly in their windscreen. They can park on the street within your allocated zone only. The permit does not allow them to park in other zones and they can't park in time-limited waiting or pay-and-display only parking areas. They must also obey all other parking regulations such as yellow lines.

If a permit gets lost

If a permit gets lost, for example if a customer drives away with it and you are unable to retreive it, then you will be unable to use that permit until it is cancelled and reissued. To reisse a permit please contact us.

Changing your vehicle

If you change your business vehicle you must update its details immediately to avoid getting a parking fine.

Renewing a permit

You can renew your permit up to 1 month before the expiry date. Renewal can be done online and only takes a couple of minutes. If you are renewing an existing permit all you need is your payment details. You must renew before the expiry date or you may be fined for parking in the zone.
